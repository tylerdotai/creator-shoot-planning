# Creator Shoot Planning Toolkit — Workshop Guide

> **How to use the toolkit. This is the onboarding walkthrough — the toolkit itself is the product.**

---

## What You're Getting

A repeatable 8-step process for planning any shoot. Walk through it once to understand the toolkit. Use the toolkit (the `docs/`, `shoot-template/`, and `skills/` folders) every time you have a shoot.

---

## Before You Start

You need:
- A camera (any type — photo, video, or both)
- A location in mind (or open to recommendations)
- The shoot template (`shoot-template/`) copied to your project
- 30–60 minutes for planning; the shoot itself is separate

---

## Step 1: Brief

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/01-brief.md`](./docs/01-brief.md)**

Capture the shoot parameters. Fill in `BRIEF.md` with:

- What kind of content (streetwear, event, landscape, urban exploration, etc.)
- Location (specific place or open for recommendation)
- Duration (how long you have)
- Gear on hand
- Must-have shots (anything already in your head)
- Aesthetic references (accounts, photographers, visual refs)
- Hard concerns (what are you most worried about on the day?)

Use the brief template in `shoot-template/BRIEF.md`. Don't skip this — everything downstream depends on what you capture here.

**Output:** `BRIEF.md` filled in with all shoot parameters.

---

## Step 2: Research

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/02-research.md`](./docs/02-research.md)**

Scout the location. You're looking for:

- Signature visuals (architecture, murals, light, textures)
- Best times to shoot (golden hour, blue hour, midday, night)
- Crowd levels (low-pressure vs. high-pressure environment)
- Access and parking
- Permits or access concerns
- Audio environment (ambient noise, PA systems, street noise)

If no location is given, recommend 2–3 options with rationale. Bias toward local options first — you might not need to fly.

**Output:** Location confirmed with time-of-day recommendation. Notes on crowd level and access.

---

## Step 3: Style

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/03-style.md`](./docs/03-style.md)**

Define the visual aesthetic. This is not "I want it to look good" — it's specific:

- What does the final content feel like?
- What references match the vibe you're chasing?
- What lens/look are you going for (wide and airy, tight and gritty, etc.)?
- What's the color story (warm/cool, desaturated/bold, natural/high contrast)?

Pull 3–5 reference images or accounts. Name specific things you want to steal from each.

**Output:** Style notes — specific visual direction, not vague goals.

---

## Step 4: Shot List

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/04-shot-list.md`](./docs/04-shot-list.md)**

Build the shot list. For each shot, give:

1. **Shot name** — one descriptive name
2. **What to do** — exact framing instruction, not "get a wide shot"
3. **Where to stand** — literally where, with what in frame
4. **What to say** — if talent is present, what do they do with hands/face/where to look
5. **Why it works** — one sentence, so you trust it in the moment

**Do not give options. Give one thing per setup.**

Always include:
- One wide establishing shot
- One tight detail shot
- One talking-to-camera or walk-and-talk
- One environmental portrait

**Output:** `SHOTS.md` — 4–8 shots max, each with exact framing.

---

## Step 5: Angle System

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/05-angles.md`](./docs/05-angles.md)**

Define three fallback angles so you never freeze mid-shoot:

1. **Wide + Low** — widest lens, low angle, catch sky/architecture above subject
2. **Medium + High** — 35mm, shoot down slightly, crop out background distractions
3. **Tight + Detail** — 50mm, find one texture element, leave everything else out

Also include one **"this angle will save you"** — a specific angle for this location that never misses.

If you freeze, go to the next fallback. No thinking required.

**Output:** `ANGLES.md` — three fallback angles + one saving grace angle.

---

## Step 6: Audio Plan

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/06-audio.md`](./docs/06-audio.md)**

For each shot in the list, decide:

**Scenario 1: Subject is talking**
→ Wireless mic on subject. Camera mic off. Shoot 30-second clips.

**Scenario 2: Subject is moving/walking**
→ Wireless mic on subject. Selfie stick or monopod mounted. Camera mic as backup.

**Scenario 3: Environmental audio only**
→ Camera mic. No subject mic. Walk slowly through the space.

**Audio checklist before you start:**
- [ ] Mic is recording (check LED on receiver)
- [ ] Gain is set (not peaking, not too low)
- [ ] Backup mic is on and recording
- [ ] 30-second clips — no single clip over 2 min

**Output:** `AUDIO.md` — mic setup for each shot type + pre-shoot checklist.

---

## Step 7: Anxiety

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/07-anxiety.md`](./docs/07-anxiety.md)**

Pre-mortem your known pain points. For each:

**If people staring is the problem:**
- No one is paying attention to you
- Walk with purpose — people read that as "I belong here"
- Shoot from the hip if you have to (low to the side, no eye contact with camera)
- Early morning = empty streets = lowest pressure

**If angles are the problem:**
- You already have the fallback angles. Trust the list.
- Wide is always safer than tight when unsure.

**If audio is the problem:**
- You have two mics. Backup is on. You're fine.
- "Good enough" beats "perfect and missed the moment."

**If you freeze completely:**
- Just walk. Find new light. New wall. Keep moving.
- Movement kills anxiety. Standing still feeds it.

**Output:** `ANXIETY.md` — your specific triggers and what to do when each hits.

---

## Step 8: Execute

**Toolkit Module: [`docs/08-execute.md`](./docs/08-execute.md)**

**Day before the shoot:**
- Charge all batteries
- Format SD card
- Pack kit
- Save location to phone maps
- Set mic, check levels

**On location:**
- Set mic, check levels first
- Do wide shots first (establish you have the location)
- Then medium, then close-up
- Then talking-to-camera (this is the main content)
- Review before leaving — 30 seconds, not a full edit

**After the shoot:**
- Offload footage immediately
- Pull selects within 24 hours while context is fresh
- Back up raw footage before touching anything else

**Output:** Shoot executed. Selects pulled. Notes on what to change next time.

## Quick Reference

### Shot List Format

```
**Shot Name**
- Frame: [exact framing]
- Stand: [where to stand]
- Say: [what to tell talent]
- Why: [why this works]
```

### Angle Fallback System

1. **Wide + Low** → widest lens, low angle, sky/architecture above
2. **Medium + High** → 35mm, slight down angle, cleaner background
3. **Tight + Detail** → 50mm, one texture element only

### Audio Decision Tree

| Scenario | Primary Mic | Backup Mic | Clip Length |
|----------|------------|-----------|-------------|
| Talking to camera | Wireless on subject | Camera mic | 30 sec |
| Moving/walking | Wireless on subject | Camera mic | 30 sec |
| Ambient only | Camera mic | — | 60 sec |

### Anxiety Protocol

| Trigger | Response |
|---------|----------|
| People staring | Walk with purpose. Wide shot. Hip-shoot if needed. |
| Don't know angle | Go to next fallback. Trust the list. |
| Audio worry | Backup is on. Check LED. Move on. |
| Complete freeze | Walk. Move. Find new light. Keep moving. |
