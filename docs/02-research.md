# Toolkit Module 02: Research

> Part of the Creator Shoot Planning Toolkit. Scouting the location before you shoot — arriving with a plan, not blind.

---

## What This Module Does

Gives you everything you need to know about the location before you arrive. Time of day, crowd level, audio environment, access — all flagged before you're standing there.

---

## Travel Planning (If the Shoot Is Away From Home Base)

> If the shoot city is different from your home base, plan the trip **before** scouting the location. No point researching a location you can't afford to get to.

When the shoot city differs from where you're based:

### Step A — Get There
Use the [Travel Agent Toolkit](https://github.com/tylerdotai/travel-agent) to research:
- **Flights** — 3+ sources (Momondo, Skyscanner, direct carrier). Target Tuesdays/Fridays for cheapest fares.
- **Transport from airport** — rideshare cost, transit options, rental car necessity.
- **Accommodation** — where to stay relative to the shoot location (minimize commute, maximize safety and connectivity).

### Step B — Lock the Budget
- Airfare estimate + accommodation estimate + transport = **trip floor cost**.
- Compare against budget. If the trip costs more than 40% of your available budget, flag it — you may need to shorten the stay or find cheaper transport.
- Flag free-cancellation options until the shoot is confirmed.

### Step C — Confirm Before Scouting
Do not spend time researching a location until:
1. You have a flight/accommodation quote that fits the budget.
2. The shoot date is confirmed with any clients or collaborators.

## What to Look For

### 1. Signature Visuals
What makes this location visually distinctive?
- Architecture (brutalist concrete, glass and steel, brick and mortar, industrial)
- Murals, graffiti, signage
- Light quality at different times (golden hour, blue hour, midday, night)
- Textures (rust, peeling paint, concrete, glass)

### 2. Best Time of Day

| Time | Light | Crowds | Vibe |
|------|-------|--------|------|
| Early morning (sunrise–9am) | Soft, directional | Low | Calm, empty streets |
| Midday (10am–2pm) | Harsh, high contrast | Medium | Brutalist shadows |
| Afternoon (2–5pm) | Warming | Medium–High | Natural warmth |
| Golden hour (5–7pm) | Warm, side-lighting | High | Cinematic |
| Blue hour (7–8pm) | Cool, mixed | High | Neon reads clean |
| Night (after 8pm) | Artificial only | Medium | Urban, gritty |

### 3. Crowd Level
How crowded does this place get at the time you're planning?
- **Low pressure** — industrial areas, early morning, weekdays. Easier to set up, less self-conscious.
- **Medium pressure** — popular spots, weekends. Manageable with planning.
- **High pressure** — tourist spots, events, weekends. Flag this in the anxiety module.

**If you checked "people staring" in the brief, bias toward lower-crowd times.**

### 4. Access and Parking
- Can you drive and park nearby?
- Is public transit an option?
- Permit or access concerns (private property, closed areas)?
- Safety notes for the area at your planned time

### 5. Audio Environment
What does the location sound like at your planned time?
- **Loud** — events, nightlife, heavy traffic → Flag for audio module. You need close-mic or you'll only get ambient.
- **Moderate** — street noise, distant music → Usable with the right setup.
- **Quiet** — early morning, industrial areas → Ideal for clean audio capture.

### 6. Location Options (If No Location Given)
If you don't have a specific location yet, recommend 2–3 options with:
- Why it fits the aesthetic from the brief
- How shootable it is (access, crowds, permits)
- Best time of day
- Any anxiety flags

Bias toward local options first. You might not need to fly.

---

## Output

Location confirmed with:
- Time-of-day recommendation
- Crowd level at that time
- Parking / access notes
- Audio environment assessment
- Any red flags for the anxiety module
