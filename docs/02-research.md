# Step 2: Research the Location

## What You're Doing

Scouting the location before you shoot. You're not going there blind — you're arriving with a plan.

---

## What to Look For

### 1. Signature Visuals
What makes this location visually distinctive?
- Architecture (brutalist concrete, glass and steel, brick and mortar, industrial)
- Murals, graffiti, signage
- Light quality at different times (golden hour, blue hour, midday, night)
- Textures (rust, peeling paint, concrete, glass)

### 2. Best Time of Day
For each time of day, note what works and what doesn't:

| Time | Light | Crowds | Vibe |
|------|-------|--------|------|
| Early morning (sunrise–9am) | Soft, directional | Low | Calm, empty streets |
| Midday (10am–2pm) | Harsh, high contrast | Medium | Brutalist shadows |
| Afternoon (2–5pm) | Warming | Medium–High | Natural warmth |
| Golden hour (5–7pm) | Warm, side-lighting | High | Cinematic |
| Blue hour (7–8pm) | Cool, mixed | High | Neon reads clean |
| Night (after 8pm) | Artificial only | Medium | Urban, gritty |

### 3. Crowd Level
How crowded does this place get at the time you're planning to shoot?
- Low pressure (industrial areas, early morning, weekdays) → Easier to set up, less self-conscious
- Medium pressure (popular spots, weekends) → Manageable with planning
- High pressure (tourist spots, events, weekends) → Flag this in the anxiety plan

**If you checked "people staring" in the brief, bias toward lower-crowd times and locations.**

### 4. Access and Parking
- Can you drive and park nearby?
- Is public transit an option?
- Is there permit or access concerns (private property, closed areas)?
- Safety notes for the area at your planned time

### 5. Audio Environment
What does the location sound like at your planned time?
- Loud (events, nightlife, heavy traffic) → Flag for audio plan — you need close-mic or you'll only get ambient
- Moderate (street noise, distant music) → Usable with the right mic setup
- Quiet (early morning, industrial areas) → Ideal for clean audio capture

### 6. Locations to Recommend (If No Location Given)
If you don't have a specific location yet, recommend 2–3 options with:
- Why it fits the aesthetic from the brief
- How shootable it is (access, crowds, permits)
- Best time of day for this specific location
- Any anxiety flags (is this a high-pressure shoot environment?)

Bias toward local options first. You might not need to fly.

---

## Output

Location confirmed with:
- Time-of-day recommendation
- Crowd level at that time
- Parking / access notes
- Audio environment assessment
- Any red flags to address in the anxiety plan
