# Toolkit Module 05: Angle Confidence System

> Part of the Creator Shoot Planning Toolkit. Three fallback angles so you never stand frozen not knowing what to do next.

---

## What This Module Does

Gives you a fallback system. When you finish one setup and your brain goes blank, you go to the next fallback. No thinking required.

---

## The Three Fallback Angles

Memorize these. When in doubt, go to the next one.

### 1. Wide + Low
- **Focal length:** Widest you have (e.g., 16mm on a kit lens)
- **Angle:** Low — shoot up toward the subject, catch sky or architecture above
- **When:** The background is more interesting than the subject
- **Why it works:** Widest lens = most context. Low angle = most power. Always impressive.

### 2. Medium + High
- **Focal length:** 35mm
- **Angle:** Slight downward — shoot down about 10–15 degrees
- **When:** Subject needs to be the focus and background is distracting
- **Why it works:** Down angle crops the horizon line. Cleaner. More intentional.

### 3. Tight + Detail
- **Focal length:** 50mm
- **Angle:** Whatever isolates one texture or element
- **When:** You need a cutaway or a moment you can't get wider
- **Why it works:** Gives the edit variety. A tight shot between two wides reads as intentional direction.

---

## The "This Will Save You" Angle

For each specific shoot, identify one angle that is specific to that location and never fails.

Examples:
- At a mural: Subject against the wall, 45-degree angle, wide open, background partially soft
- On a street: Shoot toward a streetlight or neon sign at night, backlight the subject
- At an event: Wide from the hip, crowd at eye level, no one knows you're shooting

This is your ace. When everything else fails, you go here.

---

## Transitions

Know how to get from one shot to the next. Write 2–3 transitions in your plan:

- "After the wide, move to 35mm and get closer. Don't change position — zoom with your feet."
- "Tight detail of the sneaker before the talking-to-camera. Transitions from product to person."
- "Walk-and-talk happens after all static shots. By then the subject is comfortable."

---

## Output

`ANGLES.md` — three fallback angles + one "this saves you" angle + 2–3 transitions.
