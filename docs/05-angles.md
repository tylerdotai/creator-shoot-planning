# Step 5: Angle Confidence System

## What You're Doing

Building a fallback system so you never stand frozen not knowing what to do.

---

## Why This Step Exists

Mid-shoot freeze happens when you finish one setup and don't know what's next. You look at your subject, look at the location, and your brain goes blank. This step solves that — by the time you finish a shot, you already know exactly what angle to go to next.

---

## The Three Fallback Angles

These are your default transitions. Memorize them. When in doubt, go to the next one.

### 1. Wide + Low
- **Focal length:** Widest you have (16mm on a kit lens)
- **Angle:** Low — shoot up toward the subject, catch sky or architecture above
- **When to use it:** When the background is more interesting than the subject
- **Why it works:** Widest lens = most context. Low angle = most power. Always impressive.

### 2. Medium + High
- **Focal length:** 35mm
- **Angle:** Slight downward — shoot down about 10–15 degrees
- **When to use it:** When the subject needs to be the focus and background is distracting
- **Why it works:** Down angle crops the horizon line out of the frame. Cleaner. More intentional.

### 3. Tight + Detail
- **Focal length:** 50mm
- **Angle:** Whatever isolates one texture or element
- **When to use it:** When you need a cutaway or a moment you can't get wider
- **Why it works:** Gives the edit variety. A tight detail shot between two wides reads as intentional direction.

---

## The "This Will Save You" Angle

For each specific shoot, identify one angle that is specific to that location and never fails.

Examples:
- At a mural: Subject against the wall, shoot from 45 degrees, wide open, background partially soft
- On a street: Shoot toward a streetlight or neon sign at night, backlight the subject
- At an event: Wide from the hip, crowd at eye level, no one knows you're shooting

This is your ace. When everything else fails, you go here.

---

## Transition Language

Know how to get from one shot to the next. Write 2–3 transitions in your plan:

- "After the wide, move to 35mm and get closer. Don't change position — zoom with your feet."
- "Tight detail of the sneaker before the talking-to-camera. Transitions from product to person."
- "Walk-and-talk happens after all static shots. By then the subject is comfortable."

---

## Output

`ANGLES.md` — three fallback angles with focal length and use case + one "this saves you" angle specific to this shoot + 2–3 transitions.
