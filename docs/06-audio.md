# Toolkit Module 06: Audio

> Part of the Creator Shoot Planning Toolkit. Decide what to capture and with what mic — before you're on location, not during.

---

## What This Module Does

Removes audio guesswork. You know before you arrive which mic goes where, and you've confirmed it's working. No mid-shoot "is this recording?" panic.

---

## The Three Scenario Decision Tree

### Scenario 1: Subject Is Talking to Camera

**Primary:** Wireless lapel or wearable mic on subject
**Backup:** Camera on-board mic
**Clip length:** 30 seconds per segment
**Steps:**
1. Attach wireless mic to subject
2. Check receiver — confirm LED is red/recording
3. Record a 10-second test clip
4. Play it back — confirm clean audio
5. Shoot in 30-second segments
6. Camera mic stays off (backup only)

### Scenario 2: Subject Is Moving / Walking

**Primary:** Wireless mic on subject, mounted on selfie stick or monopod
**Backup:** Camera on-board mic
**Clip length:** 30 seconds per walk
**Steps:**
1. Mic on subject, receiver on camera or in pocket
2. Use selfie stick to get camera away from body (reduces foot-step noise)
3. Walk 20 feet, stop. That's one clip.
4. Don't zoom while walking — motor noise gets picked up by camera mic
5. Play back every 3rd clip to confirm audio is clean

### Scenario 3: Environmental Audio Only

**Primary:** Camera on-board mic or external shotgun
**Backup:** None needed
**Clip length:** 60 seconds (you'll use 5–10 second cuts in edit)
**Steps:**
1. Point mic away from you (don't breathe into it)
2. Let it run — ambient soundscapes
3. Walk slowly through the space with camera
4. Stop and hold for 60 seconds at interesting moments

## Pre-Shoot Audio Checklist

Run this before every shoot:

- [ ] Wireless mic is charged (transmitter + receiver)
- [ ] Receiver LED shows recording
- [ ] Gain is set — not peaking, not too low (aim for -12dB to -6dB on meter)
- [ ] Backup mic is on and recording
- [ ] Test clip recorded and played back
- [ ] 30-second clip habit confirmed

---

## Common Audio Mistakes

1. **Trusting the mic without checking.** Always do a test clip.
2. **Continuous recording.** Nothing over 2 minutes. You'll never use it.
3. **Ignoring ambient noise.** Loud environment = close-mic only.
4. **Zooming while recording.** Motor noise ruins camera audio.
5. **Forgetting to check the LED mid-shoot.** Connections drop. Batteries die.
